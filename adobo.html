ADOBO

Adobo is often called the national dish of the Philippines. It's loved for its rich, savory-sour flavor from the mix of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and spices. Every region (and even every household) has its own twist, but the classic adobo is simple, hearty, and pairs perfectly with rice.

PREP TIME
40 mins
COOK TIME
45 mins
SERVINGS
4-6
DIFFICULTY
Easy

Ingredients

Quantity Ingredient
1 kg chicken (cut into serving pieces) or pork belly/shoulder
1/2 cup soy sauce
1/3 cup vinegar
1 whole garlic (minced or crushed)
2–3 pieces bay leaves
1 tsp whole black peppercorns (or ground pepper)
1 medium onion, sliced (optional)
1 tbsp cooking oil
1–2 cups water (adjust depending on sauce thickness)
to taste salt and sugar

How to Cook

  1. Marinate chicken or pork in soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and pepper for at least 30 minutes (overnight for best flavor).
  2. In a pan, heat oil and sauté onions (optional) and garlic until fragrant.
  3. Add the marinated meat (reserve the marinade). Cook until lightly browned.
  4. Pour in the marinade and add water. Bring to a boil.
  5. Lower heat and simmer for 30–40 minutes until meat is tender and sauce has reduced.
  6. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt, sugar, or more vinegar/soy sauce.
  7. Serve hot with steamed rice.

Pro Tip

Some Filipinos like to fry the meat after simmering, then pour the sauce back in for extra flavor and crispy texture.
