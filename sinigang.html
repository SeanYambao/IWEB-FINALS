SINIGANG

Sinigang is a sour Filipino soup made with tamarind as the main souring agent. It's hearty and refreshing at the same time, often cooked with pork, shrimp, or fish, and loaded with vegetables. It's a rainy-day favorite and one of the most comforting meals in Filipino households.

PREP TIME
20 mins
COOK TIME
60 mins
SERVINGS
4-6
DIFFICULTY
Easy

Ingredients

Quantity Ingredient
1 kgpork (ribs, belly, or shoulder) or shrimp/fish
1onion, quartered
2 mediumtomatoes, quartered
2 tbspfish sauce (patis)
6 cupswater
1 packtamarind soup base (or fresh tamarind)
as neededvegetables: sitaw, kangkong, labanos, eggplant, okra
2green chilies (optional)

How to Cook

  1. In a pot, bring pork and water to a boil. Skim off scum as it rises.
  2. Add onion, tomatoes, and fish sauce. Simmer until pork is tender (about 45–60 minutes).
  3. Stir in tamarind soup base until desired sourness is achieved.
  4. Add vegetables, starting with longer-cooking ones like radish and eggplant.
  5. Add sitaw and kangkong last, simmering just until tender.
  6. Adjust seasoning. Serve hot with rice.
