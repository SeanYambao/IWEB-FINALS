SISIG

Sisig is a sizzling pork dish from Pampanga, made with chopped pork face, ears, and liver. Modern versions use pork belly or even chicken/fish. It's tangy, savory, and often served on a sizzling plate with egg and calamansi — a popular beer companion.

PREP TIME
30 mins
COOK TIME
45 mins
SERVINGS
4-6
DIFFICULTY
Medium

Ingredients

Quantity Ingredient
500 gpork belly or pork face (boiled until tender)
1onion, minced
2 clovesgarlic, minced
2 tbspsoy sauce
2 tbspvinegar
2 pcscalamansi or 1 lemon
1chili (siling labuyo), chopped
1egg (optional)
for fryingoil

How to Cook

  1. Boil pork until tender, then grill or fry until crispy. Chop into small pieces.
  2. Sauté garlic and onion in oil. Add pork, soy sauce, vinegar, calamansi, and chili.
  3. Toss until well coated.
  4. Transfer to sizzling plate, crack egg on top, mix while hot.
  5. Serve with rice or beer.
