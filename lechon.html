LECHON

Lechon is the star of Filipino fiestas — a whole pig roasted over charcoal until the skin is crisp and golden. Known for its crunchy skin and juicy meat, it's the centerpiece of large gatherings and celebrations. While traditionally cooked whole, smaller versions (like lechon belly) are popular for home cooking.

PREP TIME
30 mins
COOK TIME
4-6 hours
SERVINGS
10-20
DIFFICULTY
Advanced

Ingredients

Quantity Ingredient
1 wholepig (5–10 kg) or 3–4 kg pork belly
1/4 cupsalt
1/4 cuppepper
2 bundleslemongrass
2 headsgarlic, crushed
2onions, quartered
as neededbanana leaves (for wrapping, optional)

How to Cook

  1. Clean pig thoroughly, pat dry.
  2. Rub inside and outside with salt and pepper.
  3. Stuff cavity with lemongrass, garlic, onions, and banana leaves.
  4. Sew up belly, skewer the pig, and roast over charcoal for 4–6 hours, turning regularly.
  5. Cook until skin is crisp and golden. Serve with lechon sauce.

Home Version (Lechon Belly)

Roll and tie seasoned pork belly with stuffing, then roast in the oven at 180°C for 3–4 hours until skin is crispy.
